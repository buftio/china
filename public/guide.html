A small Cantonese tasting guide

Eat with a little context

Guangzhou is the historic center of Cantonese cooking. The signature is not aggressive spice: it is clarity, texture, freshness and restraint. At dim sum, order several small dishes and share.

01

Har gow

虾饺 · shrimp dumpling

A translucent wheat-and-tapioca wrapper around springy shrimp. The pleasure is the contrast between a delicate skin and juicy filling.

LOOK FOR · a thin, almost luminous wrapper that does not split
02

Siu mai

烧卖 · open-topped dumpling

Pork and shrimp wrapped in a yellow skin. Richer and more savory than har gow—the classic partner dish in a dim sum order.

WHY TRY IT · it shows the kitchen’s seasoning and texture balance
03

Cheung fun

肠粉 · steamed rice rolls

Silky sheets of rice noodle folded around shrimp, beef or char siu, finished with lightly sweet soy. Texture matters more than appearance.

LOOK FOR · soft, slippery sheets with no gummy thickness
04

Roast goose

烧鹅 · siu ngoh

Goose roasted until the skin is lacquered and crisp, with fragrant, juicy meat beneath. One of Guangzhou’s defining roast-meat dishes.

ORDER · a small portion with plum sauce; eat the skin while crisp
05

Char siu

叉烧 · Cantonese barbecue pork

Pork roasted with maltose, soy and aromatics. Good char siu has caramelized edges without becoming dry or candy-sweet.

LOOK FOR · darker edges, a glossy surface and some fat
06

Double-skin milk

双皮奶 · shuang pi nai

A cool, gently sweet milk custard associated with the Pearl River Delta. Its name refers to the delicate milk skin formed during preparation.

FINISH WITH · this instead of a heavy Western-style dessert
The city is in the details

What to notice—and why

Guangzhou can look relentlessly modern at first. These details reveal the older trading city, the humid subtropical climate and the local Lingnan identity underneath.

01

Xiguan houses and lanes

Yongqing Fang · 永庆坊

Look for narrow frontages, deep interiors, wooden screen doors and shaded alleys. These homes were shaped by dense merchant neighborhoods and the need to move air through buildings in Guangzhou’s humid climate.

02

Banyan canopy and river trade

Shamian Island · 沙面岛

Walk the central avenue, then look south toward the Pearl River. The European façades reflect the island’s foreign-concession history; the huge banyans show how the imported urban plan was absorbed into the subtropical city.

03

Ancient road beneath the new one

Beijing Road · 北京路

Find the glass-covered archaeological sections underfoot. Multiple road surfaces sit on top of one another—a wonderfully literal view of Guangzhou rebuilding itself on the same commercial axis for centuries.

04

The planned modern axis

Huacheng Square · 花城广场

Stand near the center and look north–south. The library, opera house, towers, river and Canton Tower form a deliberate civic composition: modern Guangzhou presenting itself as a global Pearl River metropolis.

05

Canton Tower across the water

Guangzhou Tower · 广州塔

Notice the tightening waist and diagonal lattice. The structure is the architecture: its twisting hyperboloid form gives the tower its changing silhouette. The best first impression is from across the river, not directly underneath.

Four useful lines

Mandarin works everywhere. Cantonese is the local language, but you do not need it to navigate this trip.

请带我去永庆坊
Qǐng dài wǒ qù Yǒngqìngfāng

Please take me to Yongqing Fang.

我要去广州白云机场
Wǒ yào qù Guǎngzhōu Báiyún Jīchǎng

I need to go to Guangzhou Baiyun Airport.

不要辣，谢谢
Bú yào là, xièxie

Not spicy, thank you. Cantonese food is usually mild, but this is still useful.

可以刷外国银行卡吗？
Kěyǐ shuā wàiguó yínhángkǎ ma?

Can I pay with a foreign bank card?
